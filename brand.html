§01 — Brand system · v1.0

One wordmark. Three families. Six colors.

CivicByte reads like a newspaper of record. A serif wordmark with an italic ochre-brick "byte," set on warm cream with double black rules. Used at sixteen pixels and on a billboard the same way.

The wordmark.

§02 · Identity
civicbyte.

Set in Fraunces, 800 italic. The "byte" half is brick (#B8432B); the trailing period is brick too — a small civic flag at the end of the name. Reads as one word but the eye separates the civic mission from the technical means — by design.

Anatomy of the mark.

§05 · Construction

The construction

civicbyte.

Fraunces 800 italic. The "byte" half is set in brick (#B8432B); the period is brick. No grid, no underline — the wordmark is a single typographic gesture, not an icon-plus-text lockup.

The dot

.

The period after "byte" is intentional. It signals that the name is a complete unit — a publication, a record — not a verb or an instruction. Always present. Always brick.

Where it scales.

A voice that earns trust.

§07 · Tone

Read like a local newspaper, not a startup blog. Plain language, specific examples, attribution for every claim. When the data is uncertain, we say so. When a tool is in demo, we label it. The brand promise is the same as the editorial standard: accurate, useful, and public.
