Submit a tool
§01 — Submit

Built a civic tool? Get it listed.

If your tool is free, open-source, and solves a real civic problem, we'll review it against our four standards and — if it clears — add it to the directory.

Submission form

Tell us about your tool.

Takes about 6 minutes. We respond within a week with a review decision or follow-up questions.

01 · The tool
Must be public and under an open license (MIT, Apache 2.0, etc.)
02 · Category & audience
03 · Data & upkeep
List each dataset and where it comes from. Tools that don't cite sources get sent back.
04 · Submitter
We reply from hello@civicbyte.dev within 5–7 business days.

Before you submit

Tools that clear review tend to have these in place. Takes 20 minutes to check.

  • ✓
    Public repository with open licenseMIT or Apache 2.0 is typical. GPL works too.
  • ✓
    Data sources documented in the READMEWhich datasets, from where, with a last-updated date.
  • ✓
    Works on a phoneMost users find us from a social post on mobile.
  • ✓
    Plain-language interfaceNo jargon unless the term is what the user is searching for.
  • ✓
    No sign-ups or paywallsTools are for communities, not leads lists.

What happens next

Every submission gets reviewed by a human — usually within a week.

  1. Day 0
    You submitConfirmation email lands in your inbox within 5 minutes.
  2. Day 1–2
    First-pass reviewOpen source, working URL, sources cited.
  3. Day 3–5
    Deeper reviewUsability on a phone. A reviewer runs a real query.
  4. Day 5–7
    DecisionListed, listed with notes, or sent back with feedback.

Need help?

Stuck on a data source, scope, or the "last 20%"? Email us before you submit and we'll pair on it.

Things people ask.

§02 · FAQ

Do I have to be a student?

No. CivicByte is student-founded, but anyone building free, open-source civic tools can submit — developers, nonprofits, other students, community orgs.

Does it have to be NYC?

Not forever. NYC is where we started because it's where we live, but we actively list tools that serve other cities, states, or the whole country.

Can I submit a tool that isn't finished?

If it's in beta and usable by a real person, yes. If it's a README and a Figma file, not yet — come back when it runs.

What if my tool uses paid APIs?

Users should never pay or sign up. If your infra cost is covered by you (or a grant), that's fine. If the user has to pay, it won't be listed.

What if I can't keep maintaining it?

Tell us. We archive tools with a note — we don't silently delete them — and we might be able to hand maintenance to another contributor.

Is this a non-profit?

We're operating as an independent project while we establish 501(c)(3) status. All tools are and will stay free regardless.
