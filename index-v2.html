Why CivicByte exists
I built this for
my grandmother.

She needed to know about the violations in her building. She didn't know who to call, where to look, or how to read government documents. I searched for a tool that could help her.

There wasn't one. So I built it.

CivicByte started as a single tool for one person. Today, over 27,000 New Yorkers use it every month — tenants checking on their landlords, parents comparing school budgets, families researching buildings before signing a lease.

Every tool on this platform is free. Always. No login. No account. No cost.

How It Works
Built by developers. Used by communities.
CivicByte is an open platform. Anyone can submit a civic tech tool — and every community can discover and use them.
Step 01

Build a civic tool

Identify a real community need. Build a free, open-source tool that addresses it — and deploy it publicly on the web.

Step 02

Submit to CivicByte

Open a GitHub issue with your tool's details. We review it against our platform standards and get it listed.

Step 03

Communities find it

Your tool is discoverable by the organizers, advocates, parents, and community members who need it most.

CivicByte is open to developers, students, nonprofits, and community organizations. If you've built something that serves the public good, list it here.
