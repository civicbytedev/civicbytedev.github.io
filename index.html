Our approach

Why CivicByte exists

Public data should serve the public. The communities that need transparency the most often have the least access to it.

Infrastructure

Real tools, real URLs

Our developers are high school students who build production tools deployed on the web—not hackathon demos that disappear after a weekend.

Community

Partner-driven design

Every project starts with a conversation with a community partner—a PTA, a neighborhood nonprofit, a community board—who identifies the problem.

Open Source

Transparent by default

All of our code is public on GitHub. Civic technology should be transparent, forkable, and owned by everyone.

Build with us

We’re looking for high school developers, designers, and community organizers across NYC. No experience required—just curiosity.
