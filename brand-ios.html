The App Icon

Four-by-five pixels. Eleven white. One amber.

Drawn at 1024 · Verified down to 29

The mark is the brand at every scale.

The icon is a four-by-five pixel grid. Eleven warm-white cells form the stencil "C" — top row, left column, bottom row. The twelfth cell, bottom right, is the byte — set in amber, slightly off-rhythm. That single broken cell is the whole logic of the mark: the data that didn't make it into the right hands.

The ground is the terracotta ember, the same accent used everywhere else in the system. The corner radius is iOS-standard 22.37% of side length, so the squircle resolves cleanly at every size.

Draw the icon at 1024 px first. Verify it down to 29 px before shipping. If the byte cell vanishes at 29, the icon failed.

Component Rules

Five rules for every screen.

Short list · This is all of it
  1. 01 Editorial masthead, not a chrome nav Every screen opens with a hairline-bordered masthead. Vol/No on the left, wordmark center, mode toggle right. The iOS large-title pattern is rebuilt with Newsreader at 34 px — no SF Pro, no tab bar nav, no rounded title cards. Rule ·
  2. 02 Lists are typeset, not carded No UIGroupedTableView. No rounded white cards with insets. Rows are separated by hairline rules, numbered in mono, with terracotta arrows at the right edge. Mirrors the web's .ilist exactly. Rule ·
  3. 03 Tap targets are 44 pt minimum, hairlines not buttons Apple's 44 pt floor is honored, but the visual treatment is a hairline-underlined text label with a terracotta arrow — never a filled iOS button. The web's .cta-link pattern. Rule ·
  4. 04 The five-place ember rule still holds Italic accent word · masthead tick · key keystat number · CTA arrows · pull-quote rules. Nothing else gets the terracotta. The iOS system blue is never used. Rule ·
  5. 05 Tables compress, they don't reshape A readout that runs four columns on web becomes two columns on iPhone — same typography, same hairlines, same ember accent on the key cell. Never break a readout into stacked accordion rows. Rule ·

That's the whole system on iOS. The mark fits — the rules don't change.
