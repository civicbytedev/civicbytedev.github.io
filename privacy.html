§01 — Legal · Privacy

Privacy, by design.

CivicByte is built on a simple principle: public data should serve the public, not the other way around. We don't collect your personal information.

TL;DR

You aren't the product.

CivicByte does not collect, store, or sell any personal data. No accounts, no tracking, no ads. The tools and website are free — that's the whole arrangement.

Last updated · 2026-05-23

01 What we collect

We collect no personal information from users of the CivicByte website or any of the tools listed on it.

Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, or contact information
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Location data
  • Behavioral or usage analytics tied to you
  • Cookies or tracking pixels
  • Any form of account or login data — no accounts exist

02 How the tools work

Every CivicByte tool runs in your own browser, talking directly to the public-data API behind it — NYC Open Data, HPD, DOI, and so on. The search happens on your device, and the response is rendered in your browser. We sit in the middle as the static page that loads the code; we are not on the path of your queries.

We do not intercept, log, or store anything you type or pull back. There is no server-side database.

03 Submissions and email

If you use the Submit a tool form, that opens your own mail client addressed to hello@civicbyte.dev with the submission contents pre-filled. You choose whether to send. We only see what arrives in the inbox — same as any email — and we use it solely to review the submission.

04 Third-party tools listed on the directory

CivicByte lists civic tools built and maintained by third parties. Those tools are independent services with their own privacy practices — review each tool's policy when you use it. We do not act as a data processor for them.

05 Open source

All CivicByte code is open source under the MIT License. You can inspect exactly what runs in your browser:

  • Website: github.com/civicbytedev/civicbytedev.github.io
  • All tools: github.com/civicbytedev

06 Children's privacy

CivicByte does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13. Because we collect no personal data, we comply with COPPA by design.

07 Changes to this policy

If we ever update this policy, the new version replaces this page with a fresh "last updated" date. Because we collect no data, material changes are unlikely.
